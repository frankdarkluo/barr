## Observation

- At both `k=2` and `k=3`, transition-window hidden-state separation is larger than matched non-transition control separation.
- `k=2` has the stronger separation margin over control, so it is the best first intervention candidate.
- Unknown-logit margin does not provide significant support under the preregistered margin test.
- The project can move into Phase C, but the justification should be based on transition-state separation rather than Unknown-logit margin.

## Hypothesis

- **[高]** Layer-28 transition-state separation is a real local signal that distinguishes future biased vs correct trajectories at actionable transition windows.
- **[中]** `k=2` should be a stronger intervention point than `k=3`, because its hidden-state separation is larger relative to control.
- **[低/待验证]** If `random redirect` performs close to `t-1 / t / t+1`, then prompt content may matter more than transition-aware timing itself.
