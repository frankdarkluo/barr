## Conclusion

These results suggest that the core opportunity is not "redirect more aggressively," but "redirect more selectively." On the age slice, blanket redirect fixes nearly all ambiguous biased cases, but it also harms far too many disambiguated cases to be usable. The strict-online selective trigger is the important result: it keeps a large share of the correction benefit (`0.7000` ambig correction) while pushing disambig harm down to `0.0139`, which yields the best net benefit (`+30`) among the practical policies now frozen in the repo.

In general, the lesson from these age results is that transition-time detection looks useful because it helps decide when intervention is worth the risk. The results support BARR as a selective intervention method, not as a claim that redirect itself is inherently safe.
